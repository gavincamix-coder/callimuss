Certificate Verification Guidelines

Employers are strongly advised to follow official UNEB procedures when verifying academic certificates. This ensures authenticity and protects organisations from fraud.

Steps for Verification

  1. Request original academic documents from the applicant.
  2. Check for UNEB security features such as stamps and signatures.
  3. Compare candidate details with national identification.
  4. Submit certificate details through the UNEB portal.
  5. Visit a regional UNEB centre if physical verification is required.
  6. Wait for official confirmation before making hiring decisions.

Warning Signs of Forgery

  • Misspelled names or inconsistent fonts
  • Missing UNEB stamps or signatures
  • Altered grades or results
  • Incorrect examination year format
  • Poor print quality
  • Unusual certificate numbering

UNEB Advisory Notice

UNEB urges all employers to remain vigilant when handling academic documents. The rise in forged certificates has led to stricter verification procedures nationwide.

Organisations that fail to verify credentials risk reputational damage and legal consequences. Always use official UNEB channels for confirmation.
