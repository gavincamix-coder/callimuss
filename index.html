Background

Uganda is currently facing a rise in counterfeit academic certificates, affecting employers, institutions, and national credibility. The Uganda National Examinations Board (UNEB) has introduced a digital verification portal to help organisations authenticate academic documents.

Steps to Verify a Certificate

  1. Obtain a clear copy of the candidate’s certificate.
  2. Check for official UNEB security features.
  3. Visit the UNEB verification portal.
  4. Enter candidate details accurately.
  5. Submit the request for verification.
  6. Await confirmation from UNEB.

Documents Verified by UNEB

  • UCE Certificates
  • UACE Certificates
  • Primary Leaving Examination (PLE) Results
  • Diploma Transcripts
  • Teacher Training Certificates
  • Technical and Vocational Certificates

UNEB Public Announcement

The Uganda National Examinations Board has intensified efforts to combat the increasing circulation of forged academic certificates. Employers are advised to verify all academic documents before recruitment.

UNEB has partnered with government agencies to introduce a secure digital verification system. This initiative aims to restore trust in Uganda’s education system and protect institutions from fraud-related losses.
